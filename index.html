1. Toss Decision Distribution (Pie Chart)

Teams in the IPL historically lean towards fielding first, taking advantage of chasing under lights and evening dew factors.

2. Target Runs Frequency Distribution (Histogram)

Target scores follow a normal-like distribution peaking around 160-180 runs. Scores above 200 are rare but represent matches with high batting superiority.

3. Matches Played Over Seasons (Line Chart)

Spikes in matches correlate with the expansion of the league (addition of teams) in seasons like 2011-2013 and 2022 onwards.

4. Top 10 Teams by Victory Count (Bar Chart)

Franchises like Mumbai Indians and Chennai Super Kings lead the victory counts, reflecting their consistent performances across tournament history.

5. Target Runs vs. Victory Margin (Scatter Plot)

Matches won by runs tend to show higher margins at higher targets, whereas wicket margins are bounded between 1 and 10.

6. Victory Margin Spread by Win Type (Box Plot)

Run victories have wider distributions (up to 140+ runs) and higher variance, whereas wicket victories are tightly bounded between 1 and 10 wickets.

7. Correlation Heatmap of Match Attributes (Heatmap)

Target runs show positive correlation with the margin of victory in matches won by runs. Over time (season year), average targets have also seen a mild increase.

8. Cumulative Wins Trend for Top 4 Teams (Area Chart)

The slopes indicate team consistency. Steady upward climbs represent constant win delivery, whereas flat regions show slump seasons.

9. Top 10 Host Cities by Match Count (Count Plot)

Metropolitan hubs like Mumbai, Bangalore, and Kolkata host the highest volume of matches, serving as primary venues for multiple seasons.

10. Target Runs Density Spread over Seasons (Violin Plot)

The shape of the violins has grown taller and wider in recent seasons, indicating that pitches are producing both extreme high scores and highly variable matches.
